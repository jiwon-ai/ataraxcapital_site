# Why Atarax(ia)

I learned this word in a middle-school ethics class. Ataraxia. The Greek for a lucid calm that turbulence cannot reach. The teacher was one of those people who hands you a few sentences you end up carrying for the rest of your life. One of them was this: that every person's worth is infinite, and so all of us are equal. I have kept that sentence in me for years. In the same room, from the same teacher, came the word ataraxia. It did not enter so much as pierce. I was a child with none of the turbulence that would one day explain it, and still the word went all the way through me and stayed. So when I finally had a system of my own to name, there was nothing to deliberate. The name had been waiting for years. The firm is Atarax. I kept the calm and let two letters go.

Let me be honest about where I started, though. What I wanted, plainly, was to run an automated program that makes money every day. It is the obvious wish, and it is the wrong one. A systematic edge does not pay daily. It pays across many trades, with variance in between, and the demand for a daily win is exactly what corrupts it. The hunger for a result today is what makes you override the system on a red day, overtrade on a quiet one, and reach for things you never tested. The wish for daily money is at war with the thing that actually makes money.

So I chose the longer game, and naming the firm forced me to say what that game is. Atarax exists to build a system good enough to compound capital on its own, refined to a craftsman's standard, so that in the end it earns freedom of time, place, and attention.

Read those three freedoms again. Time and place are obvious. Attention is the one that matters most, and the one a system uniquely gives back. Attention is the scarcest thing I own, and a market will take all of it if I let it. The entire point of writing rules, validating them on data they were never fitted to, and letting them act without me, is to put my attention beyond the reach of the next candle.

Here is the part worth being exact about. Ataraxia is not reached by caring less. It is reached by front-loading all of the caring into the system, so that in the live moment there is nothing left to spend. Calm is not the absence of stakes. It is the presence of preparation.

That is why I care about the craft and not only the result. A system I do not fully trust will pull my attention straight back, and then I have lost the one thing I was building toward. For now this is built for one person, by one person, and I have stopped seeing that as a limitation. It is the cleanest version of the test. The work either holds up when the only auditor is me, or it does not.

And underneath all of it is the older lesson. A market is a machine that tries to price you every day, in green and in red. My teacher taught me the opposite, that worth is infinite and not for the market to set. So the calm I am building is partly just that, a refusal to let the tape decide what I am worth. Compounding is the patience this requires, made literal. A small edge, protected and repeated, allowed to grow without being forced. Money is the propellant. The destination is a mind that does not have to flinch, and does not keep score of itself.

> The work buys the calm. The calm is the point.
